## 3. Image Strategy Recommendation

### 3.1 The Core Decision: Text-Only Heroes for All Pages

The brand guidelines already state: **"Heroes: Text-only preferred. The headline is always the primary focus."** (Section 8.5)

I recommend following this guideline strictly. Remove the hero images from Approach and Contact. Here is why:

1. **The brand says so.** The brand guidelines are explicit. The current split heroes on Approach and Contact contradict the documented standard.
2. **The images are hidden on mobile.** This means mobile users already see text-only heroes. Making desktop match mobile creates consistency across breakpoints.
3. **The images are unlicensed hotlinks.** They must be replaced regardless. Rather than sourcing and licensing new hero images, remove the pattern.
4. **Text-only heroes match the "quiet confidence" brand.** The headline does the heavy lifting. The editorial photos in the hero compete with the headline rather than supporting it.
5. **It eliminates the inconsistency entirely.** No page has a hero image; the decision is universal and easy to maintain.

**Exception: Project detail pages keep the venture logo.** The logo in the project detail hero is functional (it identifies which project you are viewing), not editorial. It should stay.

### 3.2 Section Break Strategy

**Recommendation: Use section breaks on pages with 4+ content sections.** Section breaks are visual breathers that prevent text fatigue on long pages. Short pages do not need them.

| Page | Sections | Needs Break? | Recommendation |
|------|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Homepage | 5 content sections | Yes | 1 palette image between "What we do" and "How we work". Replace the current people photo with a Steel-palette abstract. |
| Approach | 4 content sections | Yes | 1 palette image between "The model" and "AI-first". Replace the current people photo. |
| Contact | 2 content sections | No | Too short. No break needed. |
| Partners | 2 content sections | No | Too short. No break needed. |
| Projects listing | 3 content sections | No | Grid of logos provides enough visual variety. |
| Project detail | 5-6 content sections | Yes | Add 1 palette image between Overview and Capabilities. |
| Case study | 12 sections | Yes | Keep current 3 breaks (already well-placed). Replace the hotlinked Getty people photo with a palette image. |

**Key change:** Section breaks should use **palette imagery only** (AI-generated, Steel-palette abstract). People photography should not appear in section breaks. This aligns with brand guidelines section 8.3 which states palette imagery's role is "Section breaks between content blocks on longer pages."

### 3.3 People Photography Strategy

If heroes are text-only and section breaks are palette-only, where do editorial people photos go?

**Recommendation: Nowhere on the current site.** Here is the reasoning:

1. The brand guidelines limit editorial people photos to "Maximum 1 per page" and state they are "Best on white or off-white background sections."
2. The current people photos are all unlicensed hotlinks that must be removed.
3. The site's strength is its typography, colour rhythm, and sparse confidence. Adding people photos risks making it feel like every other consultancy website.
4. The partners page already humanises the site with real headshots of real people. The editorial stock photos elsewhere add nothing that the partner portraits do not.

If the team strongly wants people photography, the single best placement would be a commissioned group photo (not stock) on the Partners page, positioned as a banner above the partner grid. But stock photos of generic "business team collaborating" undermine the brand's authenticity principle.

|------|--------------|-------------------|
| Homepage hero | No image | No image (correct) |
| Homepage section break | People photo (Getty, hotlinked) | Palette image (self-hosted) |

## 4. Other Anomalies

### 4.1 Missing Pages

**About page:** There is no dedicated About page. The Approach page partially fills this role, but "How we work" is not the same as "Who we are." The Partners page shows people, but there is no narrative about Gain Insight's founding, mission, or location. For a company that emphasises partnership and trust, an About page would strengthen the site. However, this could also be handled by expanding the homepage "What we do" section. Not critical, but a gap.

**Blog / Insights:** There is no content marketing. No blog, no insights section, no articles. For a company positioning itself as an AI venture partner with deep expertise, having no thought leadership content is a missed opportunity. The case study for Indigo is a good start but it is buried under projects.

### 4.2 Content Gaps

**Generic project detail pages.** Only Indigo has real content. Other project detail pages use generic fallback capabilities text like "Advanced algorithms process complex data in real-time." This is exactly the kind of vague language the brand voice guidelines explicitly prohibit ("Don't: leverage cutting-edge AI to supercharge your business outcomes"). Each project needs bespoke copy or the fallback needs to be significantly better.

**Partners page has no narrative.** It is a grid of cards. There is no introductory text about the partnership model, no explanation of "what does it mean to be a Gain Insight partner." The hero paragraph ("Domain experts, founders, and practitioners...") is too brief to do this work.

**Contact page has no social proof.** No testimonial, no client logos, no "trusted by" section. The contact page is where hesitant prospects need the final push. Even a single line from a partner would help.

### 4.3 Navigation Issues

**Duplicate "Contact" and "Get in touch" in nav.** The desktop nav has both a text link "Contact" and a Steel button "Get in touch" -- both pointing to `/contact`. This is intentional (the button is a CTA, the link is navigation), but it reads as redundant. Consider removing the text "Contact" link and relying on the button alone, or differentiating their destinations (e.g., button opens a modal, link goes to the page).

**No active state on nav links.** There is no visual indication of which page you are currently on. Adding `text-text` (white) to the current page link would help orientation.

**Mobile menu has no close animation.** The menu toggles `hidden` with no transition. A slide-down or fade would improve the feel.

### 4.4 Mobile Considerations

**Hero images are hidden on mobile.** As discussed, this means mobile and desktop users see fundamentally different pages. This is not a responsive adaptation -- it is content removal. If the images are important enough for desktop, they should be present (perhaps differently sized) on mobile. If they are not important enough for mobile, they probably should not be on desktop either.

**Project detail hero may not work well on small screens.** The split layout with a large venture logo on the right side needs to stack gracefully. The `detail-hero-split` class should be verified at 375px width.

**Partner cards have variable heights.** Bios of different lengths mean the 3-column grid on desktop will have uneven card heights. This is a standard CSS grid issue -- `align-items: stretch` on the grid and a flex layout in the cards would fix it. On mobile (single column) this is not visible, but tablet (2-column) will show the unevenness.

### 4.5 Accessibility Concerns

**Contact form labels.** The form inputs have `<label>` elements with `for` attributes correctly matching `id`s. Good.

**Case study gate form has no labels.** The gate form uses `placeholder` text only -- no `<label>` elements. This fails WCAG 2.1 AA. Placeholders disappear on input and are not a substitute for labels. Add visually hidden labels at minimum.

**Section break images alt text inconsistency.** Already noted above. The homepage break should use `alt=""` like the others.

**Scroll progress bar has `aria-hidden="true"`.** Correct.

**Mobile menu button has `aria-label` and `aria-expanded`.** Correct.

**No skip-to-content link.** The site has no "Skip to main content" link for keyboard users. This is a WCAG 2.1 AA requirement (2.4.1 Bypass Blocks). The `<main>` element exists (`<main class="pt-[80px]">`) but there is no skip link targeting it.

### 4.6 Footer Inconsistencies

**Email in footer uses `@gaininsight.global`.** Same domain mismatch as the contact page. The brand guidelines specify `.ai` as the primary domain.

**Footer has no social media links.** If Gain Insight has LinkedIn or similar profiles, they should be in the footer.

**Privacy Policy and Terms links exist** in the footer but the pages themselves (`privacy-policy.astro`, `terms.astro`) were not reviewed in this audit. They should be checked for completeness.

### 4.7 Technical Issues

**All editorial images are hotlinked from external domains.**
- Getty Images URLs (3 instances): Will break when tokens expire. Watermarked. Unlicensed.
- Freepik/b2bpic.net URL (1 instance): Third-party CDN. Unlicensed.

These must all be replaced with self-hosted, licensed images in `public/images/editorial/` before the site goes to production.

**Scroll reveal animations use a simple IntersectionObserver.** This is fine for performance. However, elements that start `opacity: 0` (typical for reveal animations) may cause LCP issues if the hero heading is a reveal target. The homepage hero heading does not have a `reveal` class (correct), but other pages should be checked.

**The case study gated content is in the DOM regardless of gate state.** As noted, this is a soft gate. If lead capture is the only goal, this is fine. If the content should genuinely be gated, a server-side approach is needed.

### 4.8 Content / Copy Issues

**Repetitive CTA copy.** "Get in touch" appears as button text on every CTA button across the entire site (nav, hero, section CTAs, project CTAs). The consistency is on-brand, but the surrounding copy varies between "Ready to build something?" (2 pages), "Have an idea?", "Interested in this project?", and "Facing a similar challenge?" The varied surrounding copy is good; the button text consistency is fine.

**"Kaizen" appears in the portfolio.** This project is referenced in partner venture lists and in the preferred display order, but there is no visible Kaizen page or explanation of what it is. If Kaizen is a real venture, it needs a project detail page. If it is internal/inactive, it should be removed from partner venture tags.

**The homepage tagline and approach page tagline overlap.** Homepage: "Domain expertise meets AI capability." Approach "The model" section: "Domain expertise meets AI capability." Identical phrasing. The homepage should own this line; the approach page should rephrase.
